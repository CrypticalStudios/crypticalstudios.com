What's ChainEd

ChainEd is an introductory course to CryptoEconomics that uses crypto incentives to deliver educational activities.

For example, a student stakes money which they lose if they don't complete assignments. And tutors stake for the privilege to mark work which they then get paid for, but they lose stake if they fail to mark on time.

Read on for the whole story...

Learn cryptoeconomics with our course

This course guides you through key topics in cryptoeconomics by actually using some of the blockchain techniques that it explores. So by doing the course and using its materials, you gain a practical understanding of some of the issues at work.

So here's an outline of the course in action, with buttons that let you actually take part and run the blockchain functions in question.

Warning: None of the smart contracts below have been audited. Proceed at your own risk.

Course signup

To begin, you create a publicly viewable profile stating why you want to take the course and what you hope to achieve. This helps prove identity and maintain qualification standards.

Student has to stake money to ensure you complete course. (You lose stake if you miss deadlines)

Others can stake on your behalf e.g. gift.

To pass modules, you need to submit answers to questions set by the system and also submit a new question that will be added to that system for future students to answer. Those questions are created at launch and also submitted by users. Or you partake in a relevant forum by answering a question posed by another member of the public somewhere.

You answers and questions are marked by a randomly selected group of users.

You pay to take the course and they are paid from those fees.

You gain reputation (as an NFT) by answering correctly, and they gain further reputation as teachers/markers. ZK Snarks may be used to secretly share answers etc.

Teachers/markers sign up (with a stake) to be available to work over a given course period which helps ensure your work will be marked.
